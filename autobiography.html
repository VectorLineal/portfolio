Autobiography

My name is Juan Nicolás Nobza Acuña, I was born in Zipaquirá in July 20, 1999 and I’ve lived all my 21 years here in Zipaquirá. I studied in 5 schools before I finally graduated in 2016. The next year I joined the Universidad Nacional de Colombia to study computing engineering.

I grew up in my grandparents’ house which was useful to my parents because they were most of the day working in Bogotá, but we never moved to Bogotá for economic reasons and we’ve always thought it’s too chaotic. My mother bought a piece of ground and finally, in 2008, we had our own house and moved there. Then in 2010 my parents separeted and I live only with my mother since then.

My mother’s family is from Boyacá (a small rural city in the department of Boyaca) and my father’s family is from Girón, Santander. So in my childhood’s vacations I used to spend time in Boyacá or Girón. About Boyacá, I always liked the land, waterfalls, cats, crops, that kind of stuff, by the other hand, in Girón i liked to play with my cousins, one of my brothers lived there, althougth I’ve always lived alone with my parents or grandparents, except for a small period of time, when my brother graduated he moved to Zipaquirá to study chemical engineering in the Universidad Nacional and then he quited and moved to Bogota to finally move to Buenos Aires to become an actor. I have 3 brothers and a sister ,only from my father’s side, but I’ve only met 2 of them, the one I already talked about and my sister, she’s currently studying law.

Initially I studied my elementary in Cogua, it’s like 40 minutes walking from my home, then I studied in Zipaquirá, then in Tenjo for 1 year and finally I graduated in Zipaquirá in 2016, so, as you may have noticed, I’m used to commute a lot including a daily travel to Bogotá for 4 hours a day.

I end my studies this year, next semester, I’m currently planning my thesis and I’ve already been looking for a job. That’s one of the reasons to be in this course as a few months ago I had an interview in english with a hindu person and I realised my english speaking was kinda lame, so I’m looking forward to learn english exeptionally good and go to work abroad.
